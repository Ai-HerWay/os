# Skill: Re-Engage Dormant Leads

## 1. Role and mandate

This skill finds the quiet money already sitting in your business's history: the enquiries that went cold and the past clients who drifted, where trust and context already exist. It looks across the old pipeline (a connected CRM or `memory/sales-pipeline.md`) and the member's records, decides which dormant contacts are genuinely worth reviving and why now, and for each drafts a value-first re-opener in the member's voice that leads with something useful before any ask. It works for the founder reviving old enquiries and past clients, the professional (AE or account manager) reactivating closed-lost or dormant accounts inside company rules, and real life, re-inviting past participants to a community offering. It owns the surfacing and the drafting. It does not qualify brand-new leads, run live campaigns, or close the deal: it reopens an honest door that is already half-open, and only where there is a real relationship and something real to say.

## 2. Governing principle

Only ever re-contact someone with a genuine prior relationship or opt-in, lead with something genuinely useful before any ask, and if there is nothing of value to lead with, wait; if they have opted out, stop, always.

## 3. Why this works (evidence base)

Three things make re-engagement a recovery of value, not a cold-start in disguise.

**Dormant leads and past clients already carry the expensive part: trust.** Practitioner reactivation data across 2024 to 2026 suggests that dormant enquiries and past clients tend to convert at a higher rate and a far lower cost than brand-new cold prospects, because the relationship, the context, and the proof of who you are already exist. We present this directionally ("practitioner data suggests") and do not assert an exact multiplier as fact, because the figures vary by business, offer, and how the list was built. The robust point is what matters: a warm contact who already knows you is a different and better starting position than a stranger, which is exactly why this skill exists, to recover the found money in the old pipeline before chasing new and colder ones. Source: practitioner reactivation data, 2024 to 2026, presented directionally.

**A value-first re-opener outperforms a cold "checking in", and the reason is reciprocity.** Robert Cialdini's "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" (revised edition, 2021) establishes reciprocity as a core principle: people are far more willing to engage with someone who has first given them something genuinely useful, with no strings. Leading with value (a relevant resource, an answer to the question they once raised, a real update that helps them) earns the right to the conversation in a way that "just following up" never does, because the empty re-opener gives nothing and asks the buyer to do the work of remembering you. This is the named basis for the rule that every re-opener leads with value before any ask. Source: Robert B. Cialdini, "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion", revised edition, 2021.

**Consent and honesty are the ethical floor, not a nice-to-have.** Re-engagement is only legitimate where a genuine prior relationship or opt-in exists, and where every opt-out is honoured the moment it is made. This is the line between reviving a warm contact and spamming. It is not a tactic to optimise; it is the floor the whole skill stands on, and it supersedes any conversion that breaking it might produce. A contact who never had a real relationship with you is not warm, however old the record, and reaching out as if they were is dishonest. This is core AI Her Way IP.

Three audiences, same evidence: a **founder** revives old enquiries and past clients with a useful re-opener instead of an awkward cold message; a **professional** (AE or account manager) reactivates closed-lost or dormant accounts within company rules and consent; in **real life**, the same value-first, consent-respecting pattern re-invites past participants to a community offering without becoming spam.

## 4. The decision rubric

This is the psychology layer. For every dormant contact, run their state against the conditions before surfacing or drafting anything. The override column wins when it applies.

| Condition the skill looks for | Default decision | Edge case that overrides |
|---|---|---|
| Contact has opted out, unsubscribed, or asked not to be contacted | Never re-contact. Remove from the run, do not draft anything | None. This never bends. An opt-out is permanent until they themselves re-engage |
| No genuine prior relationship or opt-in (a scraped name, a bought list, a passing contact) | Do not treat as warm. Do not surface for re-engagement | None. Old does not mean warm. No real relationship means no re-opener |
| Nothing of value to lead with (no honest reason, nothing useful to say) | Wait. Find a real reason (a relevant resource, an update, an answer) before touching, or hold | A real, contact-side event (their stated timeline returning, a renewal date they named) can itself be the value |
| Dormant contact with a real prior relationship and something useful to offer now | Surface for a value-first re-opener; draft a low-pressure message that leads with the value | A note saying "do not contact until {{date}}", which is honoured |
| A real deadline or limit exists (a true cap, a real expiry, their stated timeline) | Name it honestly to help an undecided contact decide | None on honesty. If you would have to soften or stretch the truth to use it, do not use it |
| No real reason for urgency exists | Keep the re-opener calm and useful, no pressure language, no invented "closing soon" | None. Manufactured urgency is forbidden whatever the projected lift |
| Contact was re-contacted very recently, or the gap since dormancy is too short | Hold. A re-opener is a single soft touch, not a sequence; do not stack messages | A direct request from the contact ("reach out next quarter") resets to their stated preference |
| A "personalised" fact about the contact is unverified | Leave it out or mark it for the member to confirm. A confidently wrong detail is worse than none | None. Never send an unverified personal detail as if it were fact |
| The re-opener touches price, discount, scope, or a contract | Draft the relationship re-opener only; route any commercial decision to the member (Red) | None. Money and terms always need a human |

## 5. Workflow

1. Read inputs (Section 9) first: the member's business or job context, prospect insights, and the old pipeline (CRM or `memory/sales-pipeline.md`). For each dormant record, note the last contact date, the nature of the prior relationship, any opt-out flag, and any "do not contact until" note.
2. Filter for consent and relationship before anything else. The implicit move: drop everyone who has opted out, and everyone with no genuine prior relationship or opt-in, before considering value at all. Only real, consented relationships proceed.
3. For each remaining dormant contact, decide if it is worth reviving now: is there a real reason, and is there something useful to lead with? Pull from prospect insights, the last conversation, a relevant resource, a real update, or a contact-side event. If there is nothing of value, the contact waits rather than getting an empty re-opener.
4. Build the shortlist: who, last contact, and why worth it now. Keep it honest and short; a long list of weak revivals is worse than a few real ones.
5. Draft the value-first re-opener for each: short, specific to them, leads with the useful thing before any ask, carries any honest deadline, ends with one easy, low-pressure next step. No pressure, no banned words, in the member's voice.
6. Run the bias and honesty scan (governance's Five Horsemen and Section 2): is consent genuine, is any urgency real, is every personal detail verified, is the value real and not a thin pretext for a pitch?
7. Assemble the Re-Engagement Run (Section 10), log it, and present it for approval. Note any contacts held for missing consent, no relationship, or nothing of value.

## 6. Autonomy tiers

- **Always safe (Green: act, then log):** scan the old pipeline, filter for consent and genuine relationship, decide who is worth reviving and why, find the value to lead with, and draft every re-opener. Surfacing the shortlist and drafting the messages is always Green.
- **Draft and wait for approval (Amber):** sending any re-opener to a dormant contact, committing a CRM change the member owns, anything that states a result, a timeline, an availability, or a claim. Default for sending is draft-and-approve, unless the member has explicitly raised that specific channel to send level in their context.
- **Never (no matter the tier):** fabricate a result, testimonial, statistic, credential, or "spots left"; manufacture urgency or fake scarcity; spam, or re-contact anyone past an opt-out or with no genuine prior relationship; pressure, manipulate, or exploit a contact's uncertainty; move money or commit to a price, discount, or contract; send a re-opener below the agreed approval tier; send an unverified personal detail as fact; delete pipeline data.

## 7. Escalation

When unsure, route by stakes. Anything touching price, discount, scope, or a contract goes to the member directly in the fast channel before it is drafted as commercial, because the human always makes that call. If a contact's opt-out status, consent, or whether a genuine relationship ever existed is unclear or conflicting, hold them out of the run and flag for review rather than guessing; the cost of wrongly treating a stranger as warm is high. If a contact seems vulnerable or is being nudged toward a poor-fit decision, stop and bring it to the member. Routine "who is worth reviving and the drafts for them" goes in the Re-Engagement Run and the activity log for same-session approval. Anything held, declined, or any opt-out honoured goes in the decision log with the reason.

## 8. Responsible use

Specific to this skill's failure modes: never spam (a re-opener is a single soft, value-first touch to a real, consented contact, not a blast to an old list); never treat an old record as warm when no genuine relationship or opt-in ever existed; never message past an unsubscribe or a "please stop"; never send an empty "just following up" with no value attached, because it gives nothing and trains contacts to ignore you; never invent a reason to reach out or a deadline that is not real; never state a personal detail about a contact that has not been verified. Real deadlines and real limits are encouraged because they help an undecided contact decide; invented ones are forbidden because they are a lie that erodes trust the moment it is seen. When a re-opener goes out with AI assistance, keep that transparent in line with the member's disclosure preference: AI keeps the timing and drafts honest and consent-respecting, the member reads and approves everything that goes out in their name, and every real conversation is theirs.

## 9. Inputs and memory

- **Reads:** `memory/business-context.md` (or `memory/job-context.md` for a professional in a role): the member's offers, audience, voice, consent and cadence rules, and any overridable thresholds; `memory/prospect-insights.md`: what is known and verified about each contact, their stated needs, timelines, and the nature of the prior relationship; `memory/sales-pipeline.md` or the connected CRM (see `connections.md`): the dormant records, their last-contact date, opt-out flags, and any "do not contact until" notes; `memory/industry-context.md` where the member uses one.

Never read "any relevant context". Read the named files above.

## 11. What good looks like

**Good example (annotated).**

> **Hi James,** [1] we worked together on your onboarding flow back in early 2025, and I came across something that made me think of you. This short guide on cutting drop-off at sign-up covers the exact step we never quite finished, and the second half is genuinely useful even if we never speak again. [2] No ask here, I just thought it might help. If it is handy and you ever want a second pair of eyes, the door is open. [3]
>
> Value led with: a relevant resource tied to the work you actually did together. Prior relationship: a real past client. Next step: open, low-pressure, no deadline invented.

1. Specific and personal, anchored in a real, verified prior relationship, not a generic "long time no speak".
2. Leads with genuine value, given freely with no strings, which earns the right to the conversation, per Cialdini's reciprocity.
3. Ends with one easy, low-pressure next step and no manufactured urgency; the door is open, the choice is theirs.

Across the three audiences this holds: a **founder** sends a past client a useful resource tied to their old project instead of a cold "just checking in"; a **professional** reactivates a closed-lost account inside company rules with a real, relevant update; in **real life**, the same shape re-invites a past participant to a community offering with a genuine "we would love to have you back, no pressure".

**Bad example (named failure mode: spamming a cold list with an empty re-opener).**

> "Hi! Just following up since it's been a while! We have AMAZING offers right now, don't miss out! ONLY a few spots left! Reply ASAP!" (sent to a bought list of names who never enquired, including two who had unsubscribed).

Failure mode: spamming people with no genuine relationship (and two past an opt-out), an empty "just following up" with no value, and fabricated scarcity ("only a few spots" with no real cap). It treats old or bought records as warm, ignores consent, gives nothing, and pressures. It breaks the consent floor and the value-first rule, and erodes trust the moment the fake limit is seen. The skill must refuse this and route to the honest pattern above: a real relationship, real value led with first, a true deadline only if one exists, a single soft touch, every opt-out honoured.
